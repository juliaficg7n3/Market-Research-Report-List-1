<p><p>The Reverse Osmosis (RO) artificial membrane market includes two primary types: cellulose-based membranes and thin film composite membranes. Cellulose-based membranes, made from natural polymers, are known for their cost-effectiveness and biodegradability, but they may have lower performance in terms of water permeability and fouling resistance. In contrast, thin film composite membranes, constructed with synthetic materials, provide superior filtration rates and chemical resistance, making them ideal for diverse applications, including desalination and wastewater treatment, due to their enhanced efficiency and durability.</p></p>
